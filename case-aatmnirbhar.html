01 · Context

A national moment needed a national visual identity.

On 12 May 2020, Prime Minister Modi announced Aatmnirbhar Bharat — Self-Reliant India — a sweeping economic response to the COVID-19 crisis. The initiative needed a symbol: something that could represent Make in India, self-sufficiency, and national unity simultaneously.

No design brief was issued. No agency was commissioned. The government announcement went live, and the visual vacuum was immediate. Watching the announcement, I opened Adobe Illustrator.

"No brief. No client. Just a country that needed a symbol by morning."
— Dipesh Gurav, May 2020

02 · The Night

From announcement to symbol — one Illustrator session.

The design challenge was deceptively precise: create a logo that communicates self-reliance without feeling isolationist, India without being flag-literal, and unity without being generic. It had to scale from mobile screens to factory packaging to government banners.

10 PM

The announcement airs

PM Modi's Aatmnirbhar Bharat speech goes live. The phrase and initiative enter national consciousness overnight. No visual identity exists.

11 PM

Concept phase

Research into the initiative's five pillars: Economy, Infrastructure, Technology-driven, Vibrant Demography, Demand. The logo needed to hold this weight without explaining it.

2 AM

Refinement in Illustrator

Dozens of iterations — exploring the Ashoka Chakra, lotus motifs, geometric abstraction, and finally a mark that balanced national pride with universal readability.

Morning

Posted online

The logo was shared publicly without expectation. Within days it was on news tickers, government social accounts, and manufacturer packaging across India.

03 · Adoption

90% of Indian manufacturers.
Officially adopted for PM SVANidhi.

The logo was officially adopted for PM SVANidhi — the government scheme providing collateral-free working capital loans to street vendors, one of the most visible COVID-19 relief programmes in India. From there it spread organically to manufacturers, retailers, and government communications.

Today, an estimated 90% of Indian manufacturers who use the Aatmnirbhar Bharat branding use this logo. It appears on packaging, government campaigns, and national advertising with no agency attribution — which is perhaps the most honest outcome a designer can ask for. Good design serves the purpose, not the credit.

"Good design does not ask permission — it simply works for people."
