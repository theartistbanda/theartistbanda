01 · Problem

Western wellbeing apps were being imported into a culture they never understood.

The UAE wellbeing market was saturated with apps built for Western audiences — English-first, culturally neutral, and designed around habits and frameworks alien to Gulf users. Meditation apps assumed a secular frame. Mentor content ignored Islamic perspectives on purpose, ambition, and community.

JEGO's founders had a clear vision: a streaming platform where Gulf users could access world-class mentors on wellbeing, business, and life — but designed with the cultural fluency those users deserved. The challenge was not translation. It was design empathy at a civilisational scale.

"Intuitive is always local. We had to learn what intuitive meant in the Gulf."

02 · Research

300+ interviews. Journey maps. A month of listening before designing.

Research came before every pixel. Over 300 user interviews across the UAE revealed that Gulf users did not separate wellbeing from spiritual practice, family obligations, or professional ambition — they were one continuous pursuit. This fundamentally changed the product's information architecture.

Insight 01

Wellbeing is not secular in the Gulf

Users expected content to acknowledge Islamic perspectives on mental health, purpose, and rest. Spiritually neutral content was read as culturally foreign.

Insight 02

Arabic is not a translation layer

RTL is not the mirror of LTR. Typography hierarchy, button placement, scroll direction, and reading patterns all required first-principles redesign — not just a flip.

Insight 03

Onboarding must respect ritual

Gulf users evaluate trust in the first 30 seconds. Onboarding needed to feel deliberate and unhurried — aligned with how significant life decisions are made culturally.

03 · Design

RTL first. Cultural nuance built in, not bolted on.

The design system was built RTL-first — Arabic was not a variant, it was the primary language. Every component was designed in Arabic and adapted for English, not the reverse. This meant custom typographic scales for Arabic script, which behaves very differently from Latin at small sizes.

Onboarding was designed as a ritual: slow, intentional, and personal. Rather than rushing users to content, JEGO's onboarding established a relationship — asking about goals, time availability, and areas of life the user wanted to improve — before introducing any mentor or course.

02 / Architecture

RTL information design

Redesigned every navigation pattern, content hierarchy, and interaction model from Arabic outward. RTL was the primary design language, not an adaptation.

03 / Onboarding

Ritual-led entry

Designed an onboarding flow that mirrors how Gulf users make significant life decisions — slowly, deliberately, and with intent — before surfacing any content.

04 / Streaming UX

Mobile-first viewing

Optimised the video experience for mobile consumption patterns in the UAE — vertical-first layouts, offline support, and culturally appropriate content discovery.

04 · Outcome

A wellbeing platform the Gulf could finally call its own.

JEGO launched as a culturally grounded alternative to Western wellbeing apps — with first-class Arabic support, ritual-aligned onboarding, and a mentor roster that included voices relevant to Gulf audiences. The RTL design system was not a feature; it was the foundation everything else was built on.

The product demonstrated that cross-cultural design is a skill, not a checklist. Shipping for the Gulf required going beyond personas and into lived experience — understanding how users relate to time, self-improvement, community, and faith as an integrated whole.
